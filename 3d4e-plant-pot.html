3D4E Self-Watering Plant Pot

position:
Team member

duration:
Mar 2021-Present

tools used:
Fusion 360, 3D Printing

introduction:

As a member of 3D4E, a 3D printing club on campus, this semester I am collaborating with a team of 4 to design and create a self-watering plant pot! We noticed that existing self-watering plant pots gravitated towards being functional rather than aesthetic, so our main goal of this project was to create an aesthetic and self-watering plant pot that differentiates itself from the existing ones in the market. We explored a couple of ideas, initially wanting to create plant pots in the form of Pokemons or characters. We also put thought into the type of plants we wanted to use the planters for, considering herbs, flowers, and small plants. Ultimately, we settled on creating a mini replica of Bovard College because of its iconic location behind the Tommy Trojan statue and the building’s incorporation of ivy and plants - fitting our theme perfectly!

our process:

After we’ve decided on an idea, we started going into the planning and designing phases of out plant pot! Here’s a brief overview of our process:

  1. Look into the reservoir system of the self-watering plant pot

  2. Design an initial CAD model (current stage!!!)

  3. Refine CAD model
  4. 3D print final design + use it!!

my role:

For this project, I was tasked with creating the Tommy Trojan statue! So far I’ve completed the stand for the statue and am currently working on the statue itself! Stay tuned ;)
